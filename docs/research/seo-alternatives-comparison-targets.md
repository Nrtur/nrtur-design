# nrtur — the 6 CRMs we target (SEO plan)

_Which competitors nrtur builds "alternative" + "vs" pages for, the keywords to chase, and exactly what we can (and can't) honestly say. Every price and feature-gap below was verified against the vendor's own live pages on **2026-07-10** by a deep-research pass + an adversarial fact-check. **Two standing rules:** (1) re-check each price on the live page before you publish — they change often; (2) obey the "never say" line on every card — a prior nrtur article shipped false competitor claims and we won't repeat that._

## The 6 targets (and why just these 6)

We target **one CRM per page**, each getting a "**nrtur vs X**" comparison plus a few "**X alternative**" long-tails.

| # | Target | Why it made the cut |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Pipedrive** | Cleanest fight — genuinely no native payments, no native texting/calling, no custom objects, no free plan. Great fit for our buyer. |
| 2 | **HubSpot (Free)** | The default "free CRM" everyone starts on — huge search demand, and its free tier is genuinely crippled. |
| 3 | **GoHighLevel** | Our closest look-alike (all-in-one), but built for *agencies* — we win on being simpler and for one small business. |
| 4 | **Monday CRM** | Makes you bolt on your own Twilio + a 3rd-party app to text or take payment; has a 3-seat minimum. |
| 5 | **Zoho CRM** | Powerful but it's a *suite of separate apps* you assemble; big demand. |
| 6 | **Less Annoying CRM** | Beloved for simplicity but thin on features — easy to rank against, perfect buyer overlap. |

**What we dropped and why:** **Copper** and **Streak** (tiny niche audiences — Google-Workspace / Gmail power users; not worth a page), **Keap** (weaker demand), **Salesforce** (wrong buyer, impossible to rank), and **Freshsales** (solid but the weakest of the demand set — our first alternate if you want to swap one in).

## How we actually rank (the approach)

Be realistic: we will **not** rank the generic "X alternative" head terms soon — those are owned by G2, Capterra, and affiliate "best 10 alternatives" blogs. So the plan, in order:

1. **"nrtur vs X" pages first** — almost no competition, highest buyer intent, and they convert. This is the win.
2. **Feature-qualified long-tails** — "X alternative with invoicing / with texting / for small teams," where our wedge matches the search.
3. **Get *listed in* the G2 / Capterra / affiliate lists** we can't outrank (claim profiles, gather reviews, pitch inclusion).
4. **Rival-vs-rival pages** ("Pipedrive vs HubSpot") with nrtur as the third column.
5. **Pain pages** ("HubSpot free limits," "X too expensive") — small traffic, high intent.

vs-pages + long-tails can rank in months; head terms take quarters. Sequence accordingly.

## The 6 target cards

### 1. Pipedrive — *the cleanest fight*
- **Who they are:** popular, sales-focused deal-pipeline CRM.
- **Price (verified):** Lite **$14** · Growth **$24** · Premium **$49** · Ultimate **$69** per seat/mo (billed annually). **No free plan** (14-day trial). Paid add-ons stack on top (LeadBooster $32.50, Smart Docs $32.50, Campaigns $13.33…).
- **Why people leave:** the add-ons balloon the bill (a small team easily passes $250/mo), no native calling/SMS, no custom objects, shallow automation, sales-only (no built-in marketing).
- **nrtur's honest pitch:** *"CRM + Stripe payment collection + native texting/calling + custom objects in one flat price with a free tier — Pipedrive needs paid add-ons and third-party apps for each."*
- **Keywords:** `nrtur vs Pipedrive` · `Pipedrive alternative for small teams` · `cheaper than Pipedrive` / `Pipedrive add-on costs` · `Pipedrive alternative with invoicing / SMS / custom objects`.
- **⚠️ Never say Pipedrive lacks:** Leads, Organizations/companies, custom *fields*, quotes with e-signatures, Stripe *integration*, or automation. **Only-true gaps:** no free plan · no native payment *collection* · no native calling/SMS · no custom *objects*.

### 2. HubSpot (Free tier) — *the magnet*
- **Who they are:** the default "free CRM" most small businesses start on.
- **Price (verified):** Free (2 users) → Starter **$15**/seat → Professional **$90**/seat (annual) — a ~6× jump.
- **The free tier's real caps:** 2 users · **1,000 marketing contacts** · 1 deal pipeline · no multi-step automation · HubSpot branding you can't remove · custom objects Enterprise-only · SMS is a paid add-on.
- **Why people leave:** the free tier feels deliberately crippled, then the jump to paid is steep, and contact-tier billing surprises people.
- **nrtur's honest pitch:** *"Texting, Stripe billing, custom objects and multi-pipeline automation are just included — not free-tier bait you keep upgrading toward."*
- **Keywords:** `nrtur vs HubSpot Free` · `HubSpot free limitations` / `hidden caps` · `all-in-one HubSpot alternative with payments` · `cheaper than HubSpot` · `CRM with custom objects without Enterprise`.
- **⚠️ Never say HubSpot lacks:** Companies (standard free object), a free contact store, payment collection on free (works via Stripe), custom objects (it has them — Enterprise), or SMS (it has it — paid add-on). **Don't repeat "1M free contacts"** — new free accounts are capped at **1,000**.

### 3. GoHighLevel — *the identity twin*
- **Who they are:** the all-in-one platform built for **agencies**.
- **Price (verified):** **$97** · **$297** · **$497**/mo (+ usage fees for SMS/email/calls on top).
- **The twist:** GHL **has** payments, SMS + A2P, *and* custom objects. **We do not fight on features here.**
- **Why people leave:** too complex (weeks to learn), agency-shaped (sub-accounts / white-label / reselling you don't need), surprise usage fees inflate the "$97," and individual features are shallower than dedicated tools.
- **nrtur's honest pitch:** *"The same all-in-one, but built for a small non-agency team to use on day one — no sub-accounts, white-label, or reseller complexity, priced for one business."*
- **Keywords:** `GoHighLevel alternative for small business` / `non-agency` / `simpler` · `GoHighLevel too complex/expensive` · `nrtur vs GoHighLevel` · `all-in-one CRM without sub-accounts`.
- **⚠️ Never say GHL lacks payments, SMS, or custom objects** — it has all three. Compete only on **simplicity · non-agency shape · price-fit**.

### 4. Monday CRM — *the "bolt it on yourself" one*
- **Who they are:** the project-management tool (monday.com) with a CRM product.
- **Price (verified):** Basic **$12** · Standard **$17** · Pro **$28** per seat/mo (annual), **3-seat minimum** (~$36/mo floor).
- **Why people leave:** the 3-seat minimum punishes tiny teams; no built-in phone/SMS (you paste in **your own Twilio**); its "native" Stripe only *reads* events into a board (you can't actually collect payment); automation caps; the board depth overwhelms non-technical teams.
- **nrtur's honest pitch:** *"Monday makes you bring your own Twilio and a third-party app just to text or take a payment — nrtur ships managed phone numbers, A2P, and Stripe collection built in, with no 3-seat minimum."*
- **Keywords:** `nrtur vs Monday CRM` · `Monday CRM alternative for small business/solo` · `Monday CRM 3 seat minimum` · `CRM with built-in texting / native Stripe payments` · `CRM without per-seat minimum`.
- **⚠️ Never say Monday lacks:** leads/contacts/accounts boards, custom objects/boards, quotes & invoices, or SMS entirely (BYO-Twilio SMS works). **Honest gap:** *managed vs bring-your-own* phone, and it can't *collect* Stripe payments natively.

### 5. Zoho CRM — *the "suite of separate apps" one*
- **Who they are:** a powerful, feature-rich CRM inside a giant app suite.
- **Price (verified):** Free (3 users) · Standard **$14** · Professional **$23** · Enterprise **$40** · Ultimate **$52** per user/mo (annual).
- **Why people leave:** cluttered UI + steep learning curve, weak support (their #1 complaint), and it's *fragmented* — you assemble CRM + Books (billing) + Campaigns + Desk, each paid separately.
- **nrtur's honest pitch:** *"Zoho makes you bolt on Books for payments and a Twilio/PBX for phone — nrtur puts native texting, in-app phone numbers, and on-the-record Stripe billing in one tool for small non-technical teams."*
- **Keywords:** `nrtur vs Zoho CRM` · `Zoho CRM alternative for small business` · `cheaper than Zoho CRM` / `Zoho hidden costs` · `all-in-one CRM vs Zoho separate apps` · `CRM that takes payments on the record`.
- **⚠️ Never say Zoho lacks:** custom objects (it has custom modules, even on Standard/Pro), telephony (PhoneBridge/Zoho Voice), or that it "can't take payments" (it can, via Books). **Honest gap:** *native vs bring-your-own-provider* phone, and *on-the-record vs separate-app* billing.

### 6. Less Annoying CRM — *the simplicity one*
- **Who they are:** the beloved dead-simple CRM for small businesses (top-rated for ease + support).
- **Price (verified):** flat **$15**/user/mo, **one plan**, no tiers or contracts, 30-day free trial.
- **Why people leave:** teams outgrow it past ~5 reps; weak reporting; thin automation with **no email sequences** (forces a Zapier + Mailchimp stack); limited integrations, no AI; weak mobile app.
- **nrtur's honest pitch:** *"Everything LACRM's simplicity gives you — plus native texting, payments, sequences, and custom objects built in, so you stop bolting on Zapier + Mailchimp + a texting app."*
- **Keywords:** `nrtur vs Less Annoying CRM` · `Less Annoying CRM alternative` · `Less Annoying CRM with texting / payments` · `CRM with sequences vs Less Annoying` · `Less Annoying CRM custom objects`.
- **⚠️ Never say LACRM has:** no custom fields (unlimited), no pipelines (unlimited), no automation (they shipped it in 2025), no forms (added 2025), or bad support (it's their #1 strength). **Don't flatly say "you can't text from LACRM"** — its 2026 mobile app launches your phone's texting; say *"no built-in texting platform (own numbers, A2P, logged threads)."*

## The one rule that keeps us honest

**"The mobile app can call/text a contact" ≠ "native texting."** Pipedrive, Monday, and LACRM can all *launch your phone's dialer/SMS app* — but none of them (except GoHighLevel) has a real in-CRM texting platform with its own provisioned numbers, A2P registration, and 2-way logged threads. **That** distinction is nrtur's texting wedge — never write "they can't text at all."

Same discipline across the board: say **"no native SMS/A2P/managed numbers,"** not "no phone" · say **"native payment collection, not tier-gated,"** not "no invoicing" · never position on leads/companies/convert (that's parity everywhere).
