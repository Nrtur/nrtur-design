### G001 · "Qualified" is simultaneously a LEAD lifecycle status and a DEAL pipeline stage (the classic lead-vs-deal fusion)
`modeling-defect` · **Critical** · CONFIRMED · ☑ fixed & re-audited

- **Where:** LEAD_STATUSES@8437 (Leads list/board/detail) and STAGES_DATA 'qualified' stage@7376 + DEAL_STAGE_ORDER@7969 (Deal board + Deal detail ladder); also surfaced identically as columns on the Leads pipeline board (ObjectBoard groups@7578) and the Deals board.
- **Problem (plain English):** The single word "Qualified" means two completely different things in this CRM and nothing on screen tells them apart. On a Lead it means "we've decided this prospect is worth pursuing" (a top-of-funnel lifecycle step). On a Deal it means "this specific sale has reached the second stage of the sales pipeline." Because both are labeled just "Qualified," a report, a filter, or a person reading a board literally cannot tell whether "Qualified" refers to a lead that's ready to convert or a deal that's mid-pipeline. This is the core confusion the whole model is supposed to avoid: the pre-sale lead progression and the in-sale deal progression have been given overlapping vocabulary.
- **Proof in code:** The claim's three anchors are exact. Line 8437: `const LEAD_STATUSES=['New','Contacted','Nurturing','Qualified','Unqualified'];` — "Qualified" is a Lead LIFECYCLE status. Line 7376: `{key:'qualified',name:'Qualified',color:'#818cf8',count:5,value:'$81k',deals:[...]}` — "Qualified" is a DEAL pipeline stage (a column in STAGES_DATA, the Sales Pipeline). Line 7969: `const DEAL_STAGE_ORDER=['Prospecting','Qualified','Proposal','Negotiation','Won'];` — "Qualified" is the 2nd rung of the Deal detail stage ladder.

Both progressions render as columns on the SAME Pipeline screen (pipeline-first model). PipelinePage builds one mixed `allPipelines` list (line 7741) containing deal pipelines (Sales Pipeline, whose stages come from STAGES_DATA incl. `qualified`, line 7715) AND object pipelines added via `addAuxPipeline` with `obj_lead:{object:'lead',name:'Leads'}` (line 7739). When the Leads pipeline is active, line 7810 dispatches to `ObjectBoard`, which groups leads by status with columns `['New','Contacted','Nurturing','Qualified','Unqualified']` (line 7578) — column labels built purely from the status value (line 7611-7612), no "Lead status" qualifier. The deal pipeline renders a "Qualified" stage column from STAGES_DATA. Both are picked from the same "Switch pipeline" dropdown (PipeSelect, lines 7562-7565).

Corroborating dual-meaning proof: Line 7991 (Deal detail) maps stage label→key `{Qualified:'qualified',Proposal:'proposal',...}` — the in-sale sense. Line 8531 (Leads filter tab) `{key:'qualified',label:'Qualified · Ready',pred:l=>l.status==='Qualified',empty:'Qualified leads ready to convert appear here.'}` — the pre-sale "ready to convert" lifecycle sense. Same word, two meanings. Live data carries both: Lead id:1 'Olivia Bennett' has `status:'Qualified'` (line 8444) while separate Deal id:4 'Pivot Studio' sits in the `qualified` deal stage (line 7377) — distinct entities (different names/ids), so this is a vocabulary collision, not a same-record fusion.
- **Candidate fix(es):**
    - Rename the deal stage away from 'Qualified' (e.g. 'Discovery' or 'Working') OR rename the lead status (e.g. 'Sales-Ready' / 'MQL'->'SQL') so the two progressions never share a label
    - Keep pipeline/stage OFF the Lead entirely (it already is) and reserve stage vocabulary for Deals only; give Leads a visually distinct lifecycle chip set

### G003 · deal.pipeline (name string) is never used to scope the pipeline board — multi-pipeline membership is cosmetic
`broken-relationship` · **Critical** · CONFIRMED · ☑ fixed & re-audited

### G006 · Converted records are linked to each other only by copied name-strings, not by id — Contact/Company/Deal share no foreign keys
`broken-relationship` · **Critical** · CONFIRMED · ☑ fixed & re-audited

- **Where:** buildConvertRecords, index.html:8556-8567 (contact@8562, company@8564, deal@8566); consumed by doConvert@8740 and @8880
- **Problem (plain English):** When a lead converts, the app creates up to three new records — a Contact, a Company, and a Deal — but it never stores each record's database id on the others. The Contact only remembers its company as a text name (contact.co = the company name). The Deal only remembers its company and contact as text names. So if two companies are named the same, or someone later renames or fixes a typo in the company name, the Contact and Deal silently detach from the Company that was created for them in the very same click. The three records that were born together are held together by nothing but matching spelling.
- **Proof in code:** buildConvertRecords@8556-8567 creates the three records with NO id-based cross-links:
- Contact @8562: `co:lead.company||''` is a name string; the object's only id fields are `id:base+1` and `fromLeadId:lead.id`. There is NO convertedToCompanyId and NO deal id on the contact.
- Company @8564: `company={id:base+2,...,fromLeadId:lead.id}` — only `id` and `fromLeadId`; no pointer to the contact or deal created with it.
- Deal @8566: `deal={id:base+3,company:lead.company||cname, ..., primaryContact:cname, ..., fromLeadId:lead.id}` — `company` and `primaryContact` are name strings; only id links are `id` and `fromLeadId`.

The only id links written by the two consumers are onto the LEAD, never between the three children:
- doConvert @8740: `setRows(...{...x,converted:true,...,convertedToContactId:contact.id,convertedToCompanyId:company?company.id:null,convertedToDealId:deal?deal.id:null,convContact:contact.name,convCompany:company?company.name:'',...})`
- doConvert @8880: `updateLead(l.id,{converted:true,...,convertedToContactId:contact.id,convertedToCompanyId:company?company.id:null,convertedToDealId:deal?deal.id:null,...})`
Those convertedTo*Id fields are consumed only at 8667-8669 to render the lead's "Converted to" links (`link(l.convContact,...,l.convertedToContactId)`), i.e. lead→child, never child→child.

A grep for companyId/contactId/accountId finds these fields ONLY in unrelated subsystems (ad-leads @8491-8502, meetings @18565-18568, inbox nav @9828) — never on a contact, company, or deal record produced by conversion. No contact carries a companyId; no deal carries a companyId/contactId.

Renaming/typo-fixing the company breaks every one of these lookups.
- **Candidate fix(es):**
    - In buildConvertRecords, stamp the newly-created company.id onto contact (e.g. contact.companyId=company.id) and onto deal (deal.companyId), and the contact.id onto deal (deal.primaryContactId), so the three records share real foreign keys
    - Have consumers resolve company/contact links by id first, falling back to name only for legacy records
