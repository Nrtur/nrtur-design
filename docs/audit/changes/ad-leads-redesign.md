# Ad lead sources — audit & redesign

_Implemented 2026-07-07. Audited the whole Meta/Google ad-lead capture feature (settings page, intake engine, attribution surfaces, report widget) against how HubSpot / GoHighLevel / Zoho present ad-lead sync (training-knowledge benchmark, labeled as such), then fixed the defects and restructured the page around the user's mental model._

## How it was
- **Settings → Ad lead sources** was a flat stack: source cards → form mapping → two explainer cards → intake feed → report. No overview numbers, no visible flow — a first-time user couldn't tell what order things happen in.
- **The intake feed's statuses were frozen at capture time.** Nothing ever updated a row as the real lead was worked, so "converted" counts (feed chips, Lead-sources report, "X converted so far") only reflected hand-written seed values. Every lead you actually captured stayed "new"/"working" forever.
- **Ad leads land as *Leads*, but only *Contacts* rendered the attribution card.** The record type where ad submissions actually arrive never showed which campaign/ad set/form produced it.
- **Two of the three routing dropdowns were dead controls.** The form card offered *Pipeline* and *Stage*, but the engine hardcoded `status:'New'` and never read `form.pipeline` — leads aren't deals, so "Pipeline" was a fused-concept lie. Meanwhile `form.owner` **was** honored by the engine but had no UI control at all.
- **Half the seed feed rows were dead clicks** — styled as buttons, wired to nothing.
- **Simulate was hard-broken** (pre-existing, found by this audit's headless test): the R9 phone-dedupe fix's `//` comment had swallowed the rest of its line, commenting out the dedupe consts — `ingestAdLead` threw `ReferenceError: existing is not defined` on every submission.

## How it is now
- **The page reads as a numbered funnel**: a "How it works" banner, a live 4-tile stat strip (captured / new this week / converted % / top source), then **1 Connect → 2 Route each lead form → 3 Watch leads arrive → 4 Measure & optimize** (report + closed-loop + speed-to-lead side by side). Step 2 shows a friendly hint instead of vanishing when nothing is connected.
- **Intake status is now derived live** from the linked CRM record (`adLeadLiveStatus`, id-first): lead lifecycle → new/working/qualified/converted/lost; linked contact → Customer=converted, Lost=lost. The feed chips, stat strip, and the Lead-sources report all track reality. Verified end-to-end: flipping an ad-sourced contact to *Customer* flips its feed row to *Converted* and moves the stats 25%→33%.
- **`AdAttributionCard` is a shared component on BOTH lead and contact detail** — campaign, ad set, lead form, click-ID, UTM chips.
- **Routing is honest and fully wired**: *Lead status* (applied at creation now, was hardcoded), *Owner* (new control; Round-robin respects assignment rules), *Tag* — plus a one-line explainer that a deal is created at conversion, not at capture. The dead Pipeline select and `AD_PIPELINES` are gone.
- **Feed usability**: source filter chips with counts (All · Meta · Google), first 8 rows + "Show all", linked rows get a chevron and navigate id-first (skipping deleted records instead of falling back to the wrong one), unlinked rows render as inert.
- **Simulate works again** (dedupe consts restored onto their own line) and picks a random connected source.

## Why this is better
The engine was always stronger than the page let on (real scoring, assignment rules, A2P-gated speed-to-lead, genuine closed-loop conversion reporting on deal-won). The page now *shows* that flow in the order a user thinks in, the numbers it shows are true, and the attribution follows the record where it actually lives.

## Spend / CPL / ROAS reporting (added same day)
Step 4 now opens with a **Campaign performance** table: Spend (30d) · Leads · CPL · Customers · CPA · Won revenue · ROAS, with a totals row, grouped by source or campaign via the same toggle as the bar chart. Data model: `AD_SPEND_SEED` (simulated 30-day spend per campaign, labeled as simulated in the UI) joined with **live** feed rows (leads + customers via `adLeadLiveStatus`) and **real won deals** carrying ad attribution (`allDeals().filter(stageKey==='won' && adSourceKey)`). A deal marked Won with ad attribution moves the table instantly; campaigns with leads but no spend row show "—".

**Bottom-up revenue (revised).** The first pass tagged two unrelated won deals (Forge & Co, Barrett Digital) as ad-sourced — but neither traces to an actual ad lead, so that attribution was fabricated. That was reverted. Instead, the **three converted ad contacts** (Dana Whitfield, Tariq Hassan, Sandra Iyer — real `AD_LEADS_SEED` rows with `contactId`) now have **real won deal records** (`id 9404/5/6`, `primaryContactId` → the contact, full attribution props), completing the lead→contact→deal chain and making every ROAS dollar trace to a customer in the feed. Board counts recompute from `allDeals()` (Won 5→8, +$42k) — the stage's static `count`/`value` are dead seed decoration (`dealsSeed` only reads `.deals`) but were refreshed to 8/$280k for tidiness. `AdAttributionCard` now renders on **contact, lead AND deal** detail (deal was the gap this step closed). Customers and revenue are now 1:1 per campaign.

## Lead vs Contact — routing confirmed by research (2026-07-07)
Web-verified across 7 CRMs' official docs (9-agent research pass): **Salesforce** and **Dynamics 365** land ad form fills on the **Lead** object (convert/qualify → Contact+Account+Opportunity); **Zoho** is configurable with **Leads as the default**; **Pipedrive** is connector-configurable around its Leads Inbox; **HubSpot**, **GoHighLevel**, and **Keap/ActiveCampaign** land on **Contacts** — but only because they have no standalone Lead object (HubSpot's newer Leads object is never created by ad sync). The pattern is structural: *CRMs with a first-class Lead object + convert flow route ad fills to Leads.* nrtur has first-class Leads with a Convert flow, so `ingestAdLead` creating a **Lead** (deduping into an existing contact/lead by email+phone, mirroring GHL auto-merge and Zapier find-or-create) is the correct model. **No routing change made.**

## Attribution carry-through on convert (2026-07-07)
Converting an ad lead used to copy a partial attribution subset onto the new **deal** only and **nothing onto the contact** — the contact's `AdAttributionCard` never rendered. `buildConvertRecords` now defines one `_adAttr` helper (gated on `adPlatform` — the card's own render gate; click-ids and `adTs` copied conditionally so no `undefined` keys) and an `_adHist` set (`adSms`/`adEnrolled`) kept separate because those are timeline events, not attribution:
- **New contact** ← `_adAttr` + `_adHist` (the SMS/sequence history genuinely happened to that person; the synthetic ad-capture timeline anchors at the copied `adTs`).
- **Reused existing contact** ← `_adAttr` **only if it has no attribution yet** (backfill), never `_adHist` (would fabricate SMS rows). Rule, stated in a code comment: *live ad touches last-touch-overwrite (ingestAdLead dedupe); convert is a migration, so it only backfills.* An existing ad contact keeps its first touch; the converted lead record retains its own card so the dropped touch stays reachable.
- **Deal** ← full `_adAttr` (was a hand-maintained partial set missing `adSourceLabel/adAdset/utm*` and writing undefined click-id keys). A frozen creation-time snapshot — it may intentionally diverge from the contact if a later live touch overwrites the contact.
Bulk convert shares `buildConvertRecords`, so both paths are covered. Verified headless: 4 unit cases via module-scope `buildConvertRecords` (fresh ad lead → contact full attr+history, deal full attr no history, no undefined click-id keys; ad lead → non-ad contact backfilled without history; google lead → Dana (meta) first-touch kept, no gclid; non-ad lead → zero ad keys) + E2E simulate→convert→contact renders the full attribution card. Zero runtime errors. Known cosmetic leftover: `utmTerm`/`utmContent` are card reads only seed data carries (presence-filtered, nothing breaks).
