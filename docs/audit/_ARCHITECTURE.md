## Corrected entity-relationship model

CORRECTED TARGET ENTITY-RELATIONSHIP MODEL (nrtur CRM)

Design principle: every relationship is owned by exactly ONE entity via a stable numeric/string ID foreign key (never a display name). Lead lifecycle and Deal pipeline are two SEPARATE, non-overlapping progressions with NO shared value names. A "user" is a real entity with an id. Verified against index.html (STAGES_DATA@7370, LEAD_STATUSES@8437, buildConvertRecords@8556, DEAL_STAGE_ORDER@7969, ObjectBoard@7572, ingestAdLead@21172, FORMS_DATA@16585, DealOutcomeModal onConfirm@7927).

## Key decisions (options → recommendation → why)

### 1. How to stop 'Qualified' meaning two different things (lead status vs deal stage)
- **Options:** A) Rename the LEAD status only (Lead uses 'Sales-Ready' instead of 'Qualified'; Deal keeps 'Qualified' as a pipeline stage). B) Rename the DEAL stage only. C) Prefix everything ('Lead: Qualified' vs 'Deal: Qualified').
- **Recommendation:** A — rename the Lead lifecycle value from 'Qualified' to 'Sales-Ready' (and 'Unqualified' to 'Disqualified').
- **Why:** In every mainstream CRM, 'Qualified' is a legitimate deal-pipeline stage, so changing the deal side would fight user expectations. The lead lifecycle is where the collision was introduced, so fixing it there is the smaller, clearer change. 'Sales-Ready' also states plainly what it means (this lead is ready to become a deal), and it can never be confused with a stage a sale is sitting in. Prefixing (option C) leaves the same word on screen and just adds clutter.

### 2. How records link to each other (company, contact, deal)
- **Options:** A) Keep linking by the record's NAME text (today's approach). B) Link by a stable hidden ID and show the name.
- **Recommendation:** B — every link stores an ID; the name is only for display.
- **Why:** Name-links are the single biggest source of broken data here: renaming a company silently detaches all its contacts, deals and invoices; two companies with similar names ('Meridian Agency' vs 'Meridian Agency Inc.') split their records; and 8 seeded deals already point at companies that don't exist. IDs never change, so a rename or a typo can't orphan anything. This one change closes a whole cluster of Critical relationship gaps at once.

### 3. Where the sales pipeline stages are defined
- **Options:** A) Keep the 8-plus separate hardcoded stage lists that currently disagree. B) Define stages once (per pipeline) and have every screen read that one definition.
- **Recommendation:** B — one canonical stage list per pipeline, read everywhere.
- **Why:** Right now the create form, the deal detail page, the board, the filters and the scheduler each carry their own stage list, and they disagree on whether 'Prospecting' and 'Lost' even exist. That is why a brand-new deal is created as 'Qualified' instead of 'Prospecting', why you can't move a deal to 'Lost' from its detail page, and why you can't filter for lost deals. A single definition makes 'what are our stages?' have one answer no matter which screen you're on.

### 4. What an inbound stranger becomes (form / ad / booking / funnel)
- **Options:** A) Leave it inconsistent (forms make a Contact OR a Lead, ad makes a Contact, booking makes a Lead). B) Every unknown inbound person becomes a LEAD first.
- **Recommendation:** B — all four capture channels create a Lead (status New).
- **Why:** A stranger who filled a form or clicked an ad hasn't been qualified yet — that's the definition of a lead. Today the four channels disagree, so the same kind of person lands as a Contact in one path and a Lead in another, and 'Ad Leads' don't even create leads. Making them all create a Lead gives one honest top-of-funnel, lets you qualify before spending sales effort, and removes the fused 'Qualified'/'New' deal-stage settings that were bolted onto capture forms.

### 5. Whether Leads, Contacts and Companies get pipeline boards
- **Options:** A) Keep showing them as Kanban 'pipelines' grouped by their status/type. B) Only Deals (and optionally custom objects, clearly labeled) get pipeline boards; Leads/Contacts/Companies get status views/filters instead.
- **Recommendation:** B — pipelines belong to Deals; other objects get status views, not stage boards.
- **Why:** A pipeline is a sales progression, and only a deal progresses toward money. Presenting a Lead's lifecycle and a Company's type as 'stages' on the same screen as deal stages is exactly what makes a lead's 'Qualified' look identical to a deal's 'Qualified'. Keeping boards to Deals preserves the lead-versus-deal boundary and removes the visual invitation to treat a status change as a pipeline move.

### 6. How the Won/Lost reason (and lead disqualify reason) is handled
- **Options:** A) Keep showing a reason prompt but throw the answer away (today's behavior). B) Store the reason and note on the record with who/when.
- **Recommendation:** B — persist outcome, reason, note, closed-by and closed-at on the Deal; persist a disqualify reason on the Lead.
- **Why:** 'Why are we losing deals?' is the single most valuable sales-analytics question, and today the modal collects the answer and then discards it — nothing is saved. Storing it makes win/loss analysis and loss-reason reports possible, and gives an audit trail when a deal is reopened. The same applies to disqualifying a lead, which today has nowhere to record why.

### 7. How a 'user' / owner is represented
- **Options:** A) Keep using 2-letter avatar initials as the key (today). B) Introduce a real User entity with an id; owner/actor/assignee reference the user id.
- **Recommendation:** B — a real User table with ids; one user list feeds owners, activity actors AND the permission engine.
- **Why:** Initials collide (two people can share 'SC'), and today there are two different member lists and two different role lists that don't agree — so the role you assign on the Team page never actually affects permissions, and 'Owner' can't even be assigned. A single User entity with ids makes ownership reliable, makes role assignment actually govern access, and lets a rename or color change not silently mis-attribute every record that person owns.

### 8. Whether activity history and notes are stored
- **Options:** A) Keep regenerating the timeline as demo data each render and holding new notes only in page memory (lost on navigation). B) Store activities and notes as real records.
- **Recommendation:** B — persist activities/notes; the timeline reads stored history and real tasks.
- **Why:** Today a note you type disappears the moment you leave the page, the timeline is re-invented on every render, and it shows a fake task that has no link to the real task list. For a tool whose job is tracking engagement, the history has to survive. Persisting it also lets the timeline pull in the real tasks and cross-link related records by id for every object, including Leads (which today get no related history at all).

### 9. Whether the dedicated Add pages should create records
- **Options:** A) Leave the full-page Add Deal/Contact/Company/Lead forms as non-functional shells that navigate away without saving. B) Make them actually create the record (or remove them in favor of QuickAdd).
- **Recommendation:** B — make the Add pages persist, defaulting a new Deal to the first stage (Prospecting) and a new Lead to status New.
- **Why:** The Add pages are the most discoverable way to create a record, yet they save nothing and dump the user on an unrelated existing record. That's a silent data-loss trap. Fixing them (and defaulting deals to the true first stage rather than 'Qualified') makes the primary create path trustworthy and consistent with the corrected stage model.

## Design decisions from owner Q&A — 2026-07-01

These were settled with the owner during the pipeline review. They refine the pipeline/stage model above.

### D1 · Views vs pipelines (two different things)
- A **view** = a saved filter / sort / group-by / column choice over deals. It only *reads*; it never changes a deal. Unlimited, safe. (Board-by-Stage, List, Forecast, Saved Views, Filters.)
- A **pipeline** = an ordered **stage progression** the deal actually moves through; moving a card *writes* a stage. Only **Deals** get pipelines. Leads / Contacts / Companies get **status views**, never stage boards. (Attio / Monday call the non-deal case a "board / group-by-status view", never a "pipeline" — adopt that discipline.)
- Guardrail: a Kanban column represents "what a drag writes." A Stage board writes the stage. A by-Owner / by-Tag board must write *that* field, or be drag-disabled. Never let a tracking board rewrite the real stage.

### D2 · A deal may sit on multiple pipelines — with INDEPENDENT stage fields
- A deal can be on more than one pipeline at once (e.g. **Sales** + **Customer Onboarding**). Each pipeline writes its **own** stage; the two never touch. Moving a card in Onboarding does not change the Sales stage.
- Exactly **one** placement is the **primary (sales)** placement. Forecast, win-rate and pipeline-value reporting read ONLY the primary stage. All other placements (onboarding, etc.) are **tracking-only**.
- Both Sales and Onboarding legitimately deserve the word "pipeline" because both are real ordered progressions the deal moves through — unlike Leads/Contacts/Companies (lifecycle/type, not progressions), which stay views.

### D3 · Storage: a placements LIST, not a field-per-pipeline
- Store the deal's positions as a list: `stagePlacements: [{pipelineId, stageId, primary?}]` — **one entry per pipeline the deal is actually on** (usually just one: the sales placement).
- Creating a new pipeline adds **no field to any deal**. A deal gets an entry only when it is placed on that pipeline. This is required because users can create pipelines on the fly (`addPipeline`@7749) — a fixed field-per-pipeline would not scale.
- Rules: at most one entry per pipeline per deal; exactly one `primary:true`.
- Migration note: today a deal has a single implicit `stageKey`@21152 — that becomes the one primary placement; onboarding etc. become additional entries.
- Build options captured: **(A)** for a small fixed set (Sales + Onboarding) a named field each is acceptable; **(B, recommended)** the placements list, since nrtur allows user-created pipelines. A heavier alternative is a separate linked Onboarding/Project record (HubSpot-ticket / Salesforce-project pattern) if onboarding grows its own owner/tasks/lifecycle.

### D4 · Deleting a pipeline never deletes deals
- Pipelines are containers; deals are the money. Deleting the container must never destroy the assets.
- Deleting a **tracking** pipeline (e.g. Onboarding): remove that placement entry from affected deals only. Deals + their sales stage are untouched. Safe.
- Deleting a deal's **primary (sales)** pipeline while it still has deals: **BLOCK** until those deals are moved to another pipeline (map old stages → new stages) or archived. Never silently orphan or delete revenue records.
- Current prototype gap: `delPipeline`@7751 removes the pipeline but leaves the deals orphaned (dangling `stageKey` + `pipeline` name-string, removed from every board) with no reassignment, and the confirm dialog never mentions the deals. Fix under this rule.
